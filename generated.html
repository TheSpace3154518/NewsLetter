Syria's Descent: Violence Surges, Peace Plea Falls Flat

April 05, 2025

Okay, so Syria's having a *bit* of a rough patch. Interim President Ahmed al-Sharaa is calling for peace and unity – a nice thought, honestly – but things are falling apart at the seams. We're talking clashes between Syria's security forces and loyalists of the ousted Bashar al-Assad, leaving over 1,000 people dead in Latakia and Tartous as of Sunday. The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights puts the civilian death toll at a grim 745, adding to the 125 security forces and 148 pro-Assad fighters who have also perished.

The fighting started with attacks by Assad supporters, escalating into what officials are calling a hunt for "remnants of the former regime." It’s gotten so messy, there’s even fighting around a gas power plant. UN rights chief Volker Turk wants investigations and accountability, while the US is urging Syria's authorities to hold those responsible for "massacres" accountable.

Meanwhile, things are complicated by infrastructure attacks; internet and telecommunications were down in Deraa and Sweida after a vital optic cable was damaged. Al-Sharaa insists Syria can survive these "expected challenges," and is attempting to form an inclusive cabinet including representatives from various ethnic and religious communities – a good strategy, considering how divided the country is right now. Basically, it’s a complicated situation, and the death toll is likely to climb.
